Research Instrument

Evidence Map

A clear view of which claims are well-supported, which are suggestive, which are contested, and which remain speculative.

Strength of Evidence

The map does not rank what is spiritually meaningful. It ranks how much public, checkable, methodologically constrained evidence currently supports each research domain.

Claim Ladder

Well-supported

The experience happened

There are controlled or well-documented reasons to believe people reliably report a class of experience or measurable effect.

Suggestive

Normal explanations strain

The evidence appears difficult to explain fully by fraud, expectation, ordinary memory, or known physiology.

Contested

Methods and meaning disputed

Results exist, but replication, interpretation, or experimental controls remain heavily argued.

Speculative

Possible model, thin evidence

The theory is interesting, but direct empirical support is currently limited.
