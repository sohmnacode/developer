Skeptic's Corner

The strongest version of the countercase belongs inside the project, not outside it.

Best Counterarguments

A claim about survival or nonlocal mind gets stronger only when it survives serious alternatives. These are the explanations RAI should actively test before leaning extraordinary.

Physiology

Hypoxia, hypercarbia, and crisis chemistry

Oxygen deprivation, CO2 changes, endorphins, ketamine-like neurochemistry, and temporal-lobe instability can produce vivid states. They explain some features well, but struggle with verified perceptions and clear cognition during absent cortical function.

Memory

Confabulation after recovery

People may assemble memories after the event from fragments, overheard details, expectation, and later conversations. The strongest cases document specific statements early and before external confirmation.

Culture

Expectation shapes interpretation

People often interpret experiences through available religious or cultural imagery. Cross-cultural recurrence of functional patterns matters more than matching symbols.

Fraud and incentive

Extraordinary stories attract reward

Fame, money, social status, or family pressure can distort reports. Strong investigations look for records, witnesses, and incentives to fabricate.

Research bias

Only impressive cases get published

Publication bias can make evidence look cleaner than it is. Prospective and regional collection methods are more valuable than cherry-picked retrospective stories.

Mechanism

No accepted model

Even strong anomalies do not automatically prove a soul. They may show current models are incomplete. Mechanism is an open research frontier.
