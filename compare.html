Points of Convergence Across Traditions

All traditions posit some continuity of identity beyond physical death — though what persists varies from a permanent self to an impersonal stream of consciousness.
Moral causality (karma, merit, consequence) is near-universal: how one lives shapes what comes next, across traditions as different as Jainism and Spiritism.
Most frameworks describe an intermediate state between lives — a period of reflection, review, or preparation before re-embodiment.
Children's spontaneous past-life memories cluster most heavily in communities where reincarnation is culturally expected: Druze, Tlingit, Thai, Sri Lankan, and Indian populations.
The UVA case database shows cross-cultural consistency in the types of evidence produced: verified statements, birthmarks, behavioral memories, and family recognition appear in every major case-producing culture.
Liberation or release from rebirth cycles appears in most traditions as the highest goal — suggesting a shared intuition that repeated embodiment is not the final state of the soul.
